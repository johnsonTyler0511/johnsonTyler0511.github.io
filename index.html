Tyler Johnson

Robotics Software Engineer

I am currently working for Kawasaki Robotics as a Robotics Software Engineer. I develop applications using the Kawasaki Real-Time Control (RTC) and Robot Network API. I also consult with many universities, startups and larger companies on how to use RTC on Kawasaki Robots. My work also includes developing and supporting applications that perform tasks such as automating robot configuration and purchase order checking/tracking, data logging and visualization, QA testing robots for RTC and other applications, robot programming IDE, among others. I mainly use C++, Python, ROS, and C# to solve problems. Additionally I work with vision systems (3D/2D) and other new technologies to design applications and demo systems with Kawasaki Robots.

A work project I am excited about is the development of a live body tracking robot following/teaching application. This application will use a Kinect to track a person's body and then use the robot's arm to follow the person's movements. The robot will also be able to record positions and motions similar to teaching with a teach pendant. I am using the RTC interface for Kawasaki robots, along side ROS to integrate packages easily. The ROS packages that I am leveraging are MoveIT, Azure Kinect ROS Driver, alongside ROS packages made to interface with the robot easily and abstract behaviors written by my team and I.

I have been focusing on learning Motion Planning and Controls for robots. I have gained a strong attachment for these fields and they are the main part of my Robotics knowledge I want to improve and learn more about. I have been working on side projects in my free time as well as studying these topics to round out my knowledge. I have started a Drone project to learn more about the field of UAVs and how to control them. I am also working on learning and improving my skills with OpenSource robot libraries and simulators such as ROS2, Gazebo, Robotics Library, Drake, CoppeliaSim, and more. I am very passionate about learning everything I can about robotics.

I have a MS in Electrical and Computer Engineering from Carnegie Mellon University and a BS in Electrical and Computer Engineering from the University of Florida. I studied computer vision, artifical intelligence, robotics, computer systems, and computer security during my MS. In my BS I focused on computer engineering as a whole taking a wide breath of classes on hardware and software technologies and devleopment.
